3. Enhancing Submission Stage Efficiency: Early Discontinuation Model Implementation

In this project, I developed and implemented an Early Discontinuation Model during the submission stage to reduce Freelook/Withdrawn rates. The project encompassed the complete data science lifecycle, including data fetching, data preparation, exploratory data analysis (EDA), model development, evaluation, and deployment. One key aspect of this project was the deployment of the model in real-time. Upon completion, the model was integrated into the system's policy login process to instantly determine whether a customer was likely to Freelook or not. This real-time detection capability enabled proactive decision-making and intervention, leading to a 4% decrease in Freelook/Withdrawn rates within a six-month evaluation period.

5. Welcome Calling Optimization: Predictive Model for Early Customer Churn Detection

The objective of this project was to develop a predictive model to identify customers likely to freelook or withdraw within 60 days after policy issuance. The aim was to optimize the welcome calling process by targeting only those customers who were at a higher risk of churn, rather than calling all customers indiscriminately.By leveraging historical data and logistic regression techniques, the model accurately identified 78 % customers at risk of early churn in top 3 deciles. This approach enabled the company to proactively reach out to high-risk customers, thereby reducing churn rates and improving overall customer retention. By targeting interventions more effectively, the project resulted in significant cost savings and enhanced customer satisfaction.
